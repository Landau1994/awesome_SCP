# Spatial proteomics

**Proteomics approaches that retain spatial context of cells and tissue architecture.**

## Overview

Spatial proteomics maps protein expression to physical locations in tissue, enabling microenvironment and zonation analyses beyond dissociated-cell workflows.

## Key Features

- **Context Preservation**: Maintains tissue localization information.
- **Cell/Niche Resolution**: Enables region- or cell-level protein mapping.
- **Integrative Potential**: Combines imaging with MS-based quantification.

## Workflow

```text
Tissue preparation → Spatially informed sampling/imaging → Proteomics acquisition → Spatial analysis
```

## Performance

- **Proteins per cell**: Depends on platform and sampling strategy
- **Throughput**: Moderate
- **Data completeness**: Platform- and analysis-dependent

## Advantages

- Direct links between phenotype and proteome
- Supports disease microenvironment studies
- Enables tissue gradient and architecture analysis

## Limitations

- More complex workflow than dissociated-cell proteomics
- Higher instrumentation and analysis requirements
- Trade-off between depth, coverage, and throughput
