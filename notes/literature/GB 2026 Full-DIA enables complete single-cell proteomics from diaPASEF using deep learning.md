# Full-DIA enables complete single-cell proteomics from diaPASEF using deep learning

## Citation

> [!cite] Reference
> **Title**：Full-DIA enables complete single-cell proteomics from diaPASEF using deep learning
> **Authors**: Jian Song, Amanda Momenzadeh, Hebin Liu, Chengpin Shen, Jesse G. Meyer & Xiaohui Wu
> **Year**: 2026
> **Journal**: Genome Biology
> **DOI**: 10.1186/s13059-026-04087-x

## Abstract

diaPASEF improves ion utilization and sensitivity by synchronizing quadrupole isolation with trapped ion mobility separation, making it suitable for single-cell proteomics. The authors present Full-DIA, a deep learning–driven software that enhances proteome coverage, quantitative accuracy, and analysis speed over DIA-NN for single-cell diaPASEF data. Notably, Full-DIA generates a missing-value-free protein matrix under stringent global FDR control, enabling downstream analyses without data gaps. Applied to LPS-treated and cell-cycle datasets, this matrix yields pathway enrichment results with fewer off-target and more biologically relevant pathways, highlighting the potential of deep learning for four-dimensional diaPASEF analysis.

## Key Points

- Introduces [[Full-DIA]], a novel deep learning framework to process single-cell [[diaPASEF]] data.
- Utilizes two deep learning models: DeepProfile (for 2D coelution consistency) and DeepMall (for spectral similarity).
- Employs a self-supervised autoencoder (DeepQuant) to denoise the fragment ion matrix, yielding a missing-value-free protein matrix under 1% global FDR.
- Outperforms [[DIA-NN]] in identification depth, quantitative accuracy, and runtime on single-cell datasets, leading to cleaner biological interpretability.

## Methods

### Sample Preparation
- Method used: [[diaPASEF]] (analyzed on timsTOF HT, timsTOF SCP, timsTOF Ultra, and timsTOF Pro)
- Cell type: HEK-293T, THP-1 (LPS-treated and DMSO-treated), HeLa, K562, and E. coli. 
- Number of cells: Single cells per run (for SC-293T, SC-LPS, SC-Cycle datasets) and bulk dilution series down to 0.2 ng.

### Data Analysis
- Software: [[Full-DIA]], [[DIA-NN]] (v2.2.0), [[AlphaTims]]
- Downstream: [[limma]] for differential expression, [[SCnorm]] for normalization, [[gseapy]] for Reactome pathway enrichment.

## Results

### Main Findings
1. **Enhanced Identification & Speed**: Full-DIA consistently achieved higher precursor (14% to 55% gains) and protein identifications at 1% global FDR compared to DIA-NN across single-cell datasets, while also demonstrating faster processing speeds via GPU optimization.
2. **Superior Quantification**: Using a two-species mixture (Human + E. coli) and variable loading amounts of plasma, Full-DIA exhibited better quantitative accuracy and tighter precision than DIA-NN, avoiding imputation artifacts through its DeepQuant autoencoder.
3. **Improved Biological Interpretability**: By providing a complete, missing-value-free matrix, Full-DIA distinctly grouped cell cycle stages (higher silhouette score) and recovered more specific, biologically sound pathways for LPS-treated THP-1 cells compared to the zero-filled or statistically imputed matrices generated by DIA-NN.

## Discussion

### Strengths
- Uniquely generates a complete (missing-value-free) protein matrix directly from MS signals using an autoencoder, sidestepping the pervasive missing data problem in single-cell proteomics.
- Integrates multiple dimensions of diaPASEF data (retention time, ion mobility, m/z) via sophisticated neural network scoring.
- GPU acceleration makes the tool highly scalable for high-throughput single-cell studies.

### Limitations
- DeepQuant's self-supervised training can sometimes bias low-abundance species toward high-abundance ones in artificial mixed-species data, necessitating a species-specific training workaround.
- The identification advantages over DIA-NN are less pronounced in bulk high-load samples compared to low-input/single-cell samples.

### Future Work
- Establishing Full-DIA's missing-value-free matrices as a new standard/benchmark for data completeness in high-throughput integrative single-cell analyses.
- Further exploration of deep learning models to glean deeper biological insights from highly complex proteomic datasets.
